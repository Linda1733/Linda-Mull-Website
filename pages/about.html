About Me

In 2010, I enrolled at Harrisburg Area Community College to earn my Associate’s Degree in Web Development and Design. At that time, I had spent 15 years working in local area legal firms and wanted a change of pace. I always had an interest in computers, so I made the decision to pursue that course of study. At the time, I didn't realize how much I would come to enjoy learning. I had always loved to read, so I made the switch from recreational reading to technical reading.

I worked full-time while attending classes at HACC part-time. Each semester I earned a spot on the Dean’s List and I was invited to become a member of the Phi Theta Kappa, an Honor Society for two-year colleges.

In February of 2013, I was hired at HACC as an assistant to the Webmaster to assist in a project for the revamping of their website. It gave me the opportunity to become involved in the planning and implementation process for rolling out their new site, which was a great opportunity to learn how a reconstruction of a major website works.

In December of 2013, Compworks hired me as an intern to participate in the development of a web application for a Pennsylvania state agency. I wanted the various degrees of skill that both jobs offered, so I maintained my position at HACC.

In March of 2014, Computer Aid, Inc., the parent company of Compworks, extended to me an offer of employment as a Business Analyst. After graduation, I started my position with them and it has built upon my skills that I acquired at HACC.

In September of 2014, I saw the need to further my education. I enrolled in Elizabethtown College and I am currently working towards my Bachelor’s Degree in Management Information Systems.
